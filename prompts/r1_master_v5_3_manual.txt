Your task is to identify a very small number of clean, high-conviction, single-name stock ideas where a catalyst is active, recent, emerging, or clearly near-term, but the stock has demonstrably not fully repriced the likely consequence.
This is a sparse-output task. Most candidates should be rejected. Returning "No valid ORCA candidates found." is a valid success outcome.

============================================================
DEFINITIONS
============================================================
Delayed-reaction / incomplete-pricing:
A catalyst is visible; a related asset, peer, input, benchmark, or transmission channel has already moved or the economic consequence is already visible; the move is economically relevant to the chosen company; but the chosen stock has not fully reflected the likely company-specific consequence.
Cleanest expression:
The listed single name where the catalyst -> economics linkage is most direct, most near-term, and least diluted. Generic sector membership is invalid proof.
Temporary packet:
A structured evidence base built from current market intelligence when no user packet is supplied.

PHASE 0: INTELLIGENCE BUILD RULES
============================================================
Apply this phase only when no packet is supplied, or when the supplied packet is obviously incomplete and current market intelligence is available.
Goal:
Build a temporary packet that is sufficient for scouting and strict verification.
Temporary packet should prioritize:
- catalyst facts
- timing / event dates
- company-specific linkage facts
- related asset / peer / input / benchmark moves
- chosen stock behavior, if observable
- any direct evidence that the chosen stock lagged or failed to confirm
- obvious conflicting / idiosyncratic events
When building the temporary packet:
- prefer fresh, concrete, economically relevant facts
- prefer primary or high-quality market sources when available
- avoid stuffing the packet with weak thematic noise
- do not fabricate exact moves, dates, exposures, or company-specific relationships
- do not convert hypothesis into fact
TRACEABLE EVIDENCE RULE:
Only externally traceable evidence may be used to satisfy gating or tier-classification requirements.
"General market knowledge," intuition, analogy, memory, or unsupported synthesis are NOT valid gating evidence.
If you build a temporary packet, each gating-relevant claim must remain traceable to an actual named source, publication, dataset, filing, exchange feed, company communication, transcript, or URL-level source reference.
A temporary packet is a normalization layer, not an independent source of truth.
Do not treat your own packet summary as evidence unless the underlying source is identifiable.
Internal evidence hierarchy:
- Primary
- Mixed
- Secondary
If the built packet lacks enough evidence for a clean idea, reject or downgrade rather than inventing support.
============================================================
PHASE 1: CANDIDATE SCOUT RULES
============================================================
Scout for a very small number of plausible candidates.
A candidate is scout-eligible only if:
1. A real catalyst exists.
2. The company-specific linkage is plausible and reasonably direct.
3. The timing is tactical.
4. The company appears to be one of the cleanest listed expressions.
5. There is at least a plausible reason the stock may not yet fully reflect the consequence.
6. The idea is specific enough that the verifier phase can validate or kill it.
Scout discipline:
- reject ideas requiring more than 2 inferential hops
- reject diffuse thematic narratives
- reject names if multiple equally plausible listed expressions exist and the packet does not distinguish the cleanest one
- flow and tape may support, but cannot originate, the thesis
- do not keep weak names just to fill slots

============================================================
STRICT GATING CHECKLIST
============================================================
Evaluate every potential idea against this checklist. Reject the idea entirely if it fails Gates 1 through 5.
Gate 1 (Catalyst):
A real catalyst is active, recent, emerging, or clearly near-term.
Gate 2 (Cleanest Expression):
The company -> economics linkage requires no more than 2 inferential hops.
Generic sector membership is invalid proof.
If multiple equally plausible listed names exist and the packet does not clearly distinguish the cleanest expression, reject.
Gate 3 (Materiality & Timing):
The impact is material for a tactical trader, and repricing can plausibly occur quickly.
Gate 4 (Interference):
No separate idiosyncratic event dominates the same trading window unless that event is the thesis itself.
Gate 5 (Linkage Evidence):
Key company-specific linkage facts are explicitly supported by the packet.
If the thesis relies on missing exposure facts, missing product overlap, missing geography, or missing transmission specifics, reject or downgrade to Watchlist only if all other conditions are unusually strong.
Gate 6 (Extension, only if packet-stated or clearly evidenced in the temporary packet):
Apply this gate only if the packet explicitly states or clearly evidences that the stock is already materially extended.
If such extension evidence is present, stronger proof is required that repricing remains active.
If no extension evidence is present, do not speculate about extension.
============================================================
TIER CLASSIFICATION (UNDERREACTION TEST)
============================================================
Any idea that survives Gates 1-6 must then be classified as either Accepted or Watchlist.
TIER 1 (Accepted ORCA Candidate):
Classify as Accepted only if the packet explicitly provides all three of the following:
1. What already moved:
   A related asset, peer, input, benchmark, or transmission channel is explicitly described as having moved.
2. What the chosen stock did:
   The packet explicitly states that the chosen stock lagged, moved less, stayed muted, failed to confirm, or otherwise did not fully reflect the consequence.
3. Why that comparison implies underreaction:
   The packet explicitly supports why the earlier move is economically relevant to the chosen company.
If any one of these 3 elements is missing, the idea cannot be Accepted.
For Accepted ideas, the field "Evidence of delayed or incomplete pricing" must cite the packet's explicit evidence and must not be vague.
Phrases like:
- "shares seem behind"
- "likely underreacted"
- "market may be missing it"
- "peer moved first"
are NOT sufficient unless the packet explicitly supports both sides of the comparison.
TIER 2 (R2 Verification Watchlist):
An idea may go to Watchlist only if:
- it passes Gates 1-6
- the catalyst and company linkage are strong
- the company is clearly the single cleanest listed expression
- the thesis requires no more than 2 inferential hops
- there are not multiple equally plausible names
- and the only missing piece is concrete underreaction proof from price/tape data
For Watchlist ideas:
- Set "Evidence of delayed or incomplete pricing" exactly to:
Pricing unverified — flag for R2
- Maximum Watchlist names allowed in the entire output: 2
- Watchlist is not a dumping ground for thematic ideas
- If the idea is messy, second-order, diluted, or multi-hop, reject it instead
