IDEA #2
Tier: Accepted ORCA Candidate
Packet origin: HUNT-built
Source quality: Mixed
Ticker | Company | Direction: DHT | DHT Holdings, Inc. | Bullish
Catalyst: On April 2, 2026, the US President explicitly reversed course on Iran peace signals, promising to hit Iran "extremely hard" over the next 2-3 weeks and extending the closure of the Strait of Hormuz.
Catalyst timing: April 2, 2026 | Status: Active
Repricing window: days to 3 weeks
Entry trigger: Breakout and daily close above the 52-week high of $20.55.
Invalidation condition: Reopening of the Strait of Hormuz to normal tanker traffic, or a collapse in Gulf Coast spot freight rates back toward historical averages.
Target / repricing path: Repricing toward multi-year highs as the company's time-charter coverage locks in the 5x spot rate expansion.
Horizon / time stop: 3 to 4 weeks
Cleanest expression: DHT is a pure-play crude tanker owner actively capitalizing on the tightened export market, directly benefiting as Asian and European buyers scramble for US Gulf Coast replacement barrels amid Middle East supply cuts.
What already moved: Gulf Coast VLCC availability halved, and spot freight rates for Suezmax/Aframax surged 500% from an average of $60,000 to upwards of $300,000 per day.
Evidence of lag: Despite physical spot rates surging 5x, the gathered evidence explicitly shows DHT shares closed at $18.11 and traded near $18.76 on April 2, remaining roughly 9% below their 52-week high of $20.55.
Thesis: The Strait of Hormuz closure has structurally disrupted the global oil supply chain, cratering tanker availability along the US Gulf Coast and sending day rates soaring 500% to $300k/day. While the physical tanker market has violently repriced this scarcity, tanker equities like DHT have lagged, trading below their 52-week highs because equity investors assumed a quick ceasefire. As the market digests that the war is extending following today's political reversal, the equity should catch up to the physical rate environment.
Counter-thesis: The crude futures market remains backwardated and expects a peace deal by mid-year, meaning the market views the rate spike as highly transient and is unwilling to capitalize it into equity valuations.
R2 flags: Verify current spot VLCC rate quotes, track US Gulf Coast export volumes, and check insider selling at elevated levels.
Key sources: Charles Schwab; U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA); Marine Link

IDEA #3
Tier: Accepted ORCA Candidate
Packet origin: HUNT-built
Source quality: Mixed
Ticker | Company | Direction: MOS | The Mosaic Company | Bearish
Catalyst: QatarEnergy set a record April 2026 sulphur contract price of $570/tonne (and ADNOC at $600/tonne) due to the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz to commercial shipping.
Catalyst timing: April 1, 2026 | Status: Recently Triggered
Repricing window: days to 3 weeks
Entry trigger: Breakdown below the 50-day moving average and failure to hold the $25.00 support level.
Invalidation condition: The Strait of Hormuz reopens quickly to Western shipping, collapsing sulphur premiums, or global phosphate end-product prices surge enough to fully offset the record input costs.
Target / repricing path: Reversal of the YTD sector-sympathy rally, targeting a return toward pre-conflict baseline levels as margin reality sets in.
Horizon / time stop: 3 to 4 weeks
Cleanest expression: Mosaic is heavily dependent on sulphur as a key raw material for its phosphate production, exposing it directly to this specific input cost spike, unlike insulated pure-nitrogen producers.
What already moved: QatarEnergy and ADNOC explicitly raised April 2026 sulphur prices to record highs ($570-$600/tonne). Pure-play nitrogen peers like CF Industries surged nearly 60% YTD on fertilizer supply shocks.
Evidence of lag: Despite the specific headwind to its phosphate margins, Barchart explicitly notes MOS shares are "lagging the rest of the industry, with an approximately 18% YTD gain" and are still "bouncing between the 50-day and 200-day moving averages," retaining a sympathetic premium.
Thesis: The effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz has stranded Middle East exports, causing April 2026 sulphur contract prices to hit a record $570-$600 per tonne. Because sulphur is a primary input for Mosaic's phosphate business, this shock creates a severe margin squeeze. However, Mosaic shares are still up ~18% year-to-date, rallying sympathetically with the broader "Hormuz boosts fertilizers" narrative alongside insulated nitrogen peers. The stock has not yet decoupled to reflect the specific, direct cost-of-goods-sold damage it will sustain from record sulphur prices, setting up a clean bearish repricing.
Counter-thesis: The market may be correctly anticipating that Mosaic can fully absorb the input costs because global demand for phosphate remains strong and rising wholesale phosphate prices are occurring simultaneously.
R2 flags: Verify current phosphate-to-sulphur margin spread, exact sulphur contract exposure, Q1 earnings date, and short borrow availability.
Key sources: Discovery Alert; Barchart; Zacks Investment Research
Correlation note: DHT and MOS share the same root macro catalyst (the US-Iran conflict and Strait of Hormuz closure). DHT is a bullish expression of the resulting tanker capacity squeeze, while MOS is a bearish expression of the resulting input cost (sulphur) spike.
