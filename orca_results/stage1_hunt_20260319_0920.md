Nutrien makes most of its products in the United States and Canada, meaning its production is entirely insulated from the Middle Eastern blockade while its product prices capture the global supply-driven spike. For 2026, Nutrien forecasts potash sales of 14.1–14.8M tons and nitrogen sales of 9.2–9.7M tons. Unlike CF Industries (purer nitrogen but facing a Senator Hawley price-gouging accusation as an idiosyncratic overhang) or Mosaic (primarily phosphate/potash, less nitrogen exposure), NTR combines the broadest fertilizer exposure with the cleanest idiosyncratic risk profile. NTR trades at a forward P/E of 15, lower than the sector median of 17, and its nitrogen, potash, and retail segments all benefit from the current environment.
