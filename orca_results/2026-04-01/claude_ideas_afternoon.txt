Cleanest expression: Micron is the only US-listed pure-play memory semiconductor manufacturer. Samsung and SK Hynix are Korean-listed. Micron's business is now concentrated on AI-optimized memory after exiting the consumer PC memory market. Revenue exposure to HBM and data center DRAM is direct and undiluted by unrelated business lines. Forward P/E below 4x on FY2027 analyst estimates of ~$98 EPS.
What already moved: Direct memory peers Samsung Electronics surged 13.6% and SK Hynix surged 11.4% on April 1, 2026 (Trading Economics, TradingKey). Bernstein on March 31 called the TurboQuant-driven memory sell-off "overdone." Broad semiconductor stocks outperformed the market on April 1, with the Nasdaq up 1.16% versus S&P 500 up 0.72%.
Evidence of lag: Pricing unverified — flag for R2
Thesis: Micron reported the strongest quarter in memory industry history on March 18, tripling revenue and beating EPS estimates by 33%, while simultaneously announcing HBM4 mass production for NVIDIA's flagship AI platform with fully contracted capacity. Despite this, MU has fallen approximately 22% from its March 18 ATH of $471 to approximately $368, driven by a combination of war-related risk-off and TurboQuant compression fears that multiple analysts now call overblown. Korean memory peers bounced sharply on April 1 as geopolitical risk receded, but MU's recovery relative to its earnings-driven fundamental repricing remains incomplete. R2 must verify whether the ~22% discount to ATH reflects genuine fundamental risk or a recoverable sentiment overshoot.
Counter-thesis: Citi cut its MU price target from $510 to $425 on April 1, citing a 6% decline in mainstream DDR5 16GB DRAM spot prices since the March 18 earnings report. If the Google TurboQuant memory compression technique proves viable at scale, it could structurally reduce HBM demand per GPU, undermining the core shortage thesis that supports MU's premium valuation. DRAM spot softness may signal the pricing upcycle has peaked in the commodity segment.
R2 flags: Verify TurboQuant technical validity and potential HBM demand displacement. Verify DRAM spot price trajectory vs HBM contract pricing (contracted vs spot exposure mix). Confirm MU's April 1 price action relative to Korean peer bounce magnitude after adjusting for Korea-specific dynamics. Check whether war-related risk premium has fully unwound from MU or whether further recovery is plausible. Monitor Citi's DDR5 spot price data for continued softness.
