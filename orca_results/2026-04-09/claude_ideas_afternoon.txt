Ticker | Company | Direction: CF | CF Industries Holdings | Bullish
Catalyst: The US-Iran two-week ceasefire announced April 8 triggered a sharp sell-off in North American fertilizer names on the assumption that the Strait of Hormuz would reopen and normalize fertilizer supply. As of April 9, Hormuz remains effectively blocked - only 4 vessel transits recorded on the ceasefire day versus a pre-war norm of 135 daily, more than 400 oil tankers and dozens of LNG/LPG carriers remain anchored outside the Gulf, and major shipping lines including Hapag-Lloyd and Maersk are refusing to transit. Oil repriced the reality (WTI bounced 6% to ~$100 on April 9) but CF has not proportionally recovered.
Catalyst timing: 2026-04-08 (ceasefire announced) through 2026-04-09 (Hormuz confirmed still blocked) | Status: Active
Repricing window: days to 2 weeks (aligned with the ceasefire window and first scheduled negotiations in Islamabad on April 12)
Entry trigger: CF holds above its April 8 intraday low (~$110) and WTI sustains above $95, confirming Hormuz supply disruption is not normalizing
Invalidation condition: Meaningful resumption of tanker traffic through Hormuz (dozens of daily transits, not single digits), or ceasefire leads to confirmed full Hormuz reopening with shipping insurers restoring normal war-risk coverage
Target / repricing path: Partial recovery of the ceasefire gap toward $130-135 if Hormuz normalization stalls, which represents recapturing roughly half the April 8 sell-off
Horizon / time stop: 1-2 weeks, or exit earlier if ceasefire negotiations produce a verified Hormuz reopening with confirmed tanker traffic normalization
Cleanest expression: CF Industries is the purest listed North American nitrogen producer. Its production is overwhelmingly domestic (US shale gas feedstock), meaning it directly benefits from elevated global urea prices without the supply-chain disruption that hurts competitors reliant on Gulf feedstocks. Urea prices remain up approximately 40% since the start of the war. Multiple analysts (Morgan Stanley, BNP Paribas, RBC) have raised CF price targets citing the Hormuz-driven nitrogen supply disruption. Nutrien is more diversified across potash and retail; Mosaic is primarily phosphate/potash. CF is the cleanest single-name nitrogen beneficiary.
What already moved: WTI crude bounced approximately 6% on April 9 to above $100 per barrel after dropping 16% on the ceasefire headline, as the market recognized that Hormuz remains largely blocked. CNBC, Bloomberg, and S&P Global all reported only 3-4 vessel transits and continued shipping refusals on April 9.
Evidence of lag: CF dropped approximately 8-12% from its April 7 close (~$134) to the $117-123 range on the ceasefire headline, while oil has already bounced meaningfully higher. The physical reality underlying CF's earnings - fertilizer supply disruption from Hormuz blockade, urea prices up 40%, and shipping normalization estimated at "weeks if not months" by industry executives - has not changed, yet CF continues to carry the ceasefire discount.
Thesis: The ceasefire headline triggered a reflexive sell-off in fertilizer names on the assumption that Hormuz reopening and supply normalization would follow quickly. The physical evidence from April 8-9 decisively contradicts that assumption: Hormuz remains blocked, shipping companies are refusing to transit, and industry experts project weeks-to-months normalization even in an optimistic scenario. Oil has already repriced this reality, but CF has not proportionally recovered. If the ceasefire collapses or Hormuz remains functionally blocked through the negotiation window, CF's ceasefire discount closes rapidly.
Counter-thesis: The USDA Prospective Plantings report (March 31) showed US farmers pivoting acreage from corn to soybeans in response to elevated fertilizer costs, directly reducing domestic nitrogen demand. CF at $117 still trades approximately 75% above its pre-war price (~$67), meaning substantial crisis premium remains embedded even after the sell-off. If the ceasefire does hold and leads to a permanent deal with verified Hormuz reopening, CF faces a much steeper correction as 30%+ of global urea supply re-enters the market.

Earnings May 6 - outside the repricing window but monitor for pre-earnings positioning effects. Check options vol surface for ceasefire-related skew. Verify that the corn-to-soybean pivot has not already materially reduced near-term nitrogen demand at the wholesale level.
