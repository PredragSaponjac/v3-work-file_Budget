Cleanest expression: CF Industries is the purest-play US-listed nitrogen producer, with North American and UK production facilities entirely insulated from the Hormuz physical blockade while selling into a global market where 35% of seaborne urea and 50% of seaborne ammonia are effectively stranded. Its cost base is pinned to low Henry Hub natural gas ($4/MMBtu range) while urea sells at $692-734/st NOLA.
What already moved: NOLA urea barges hit a four-year high of $734/st on April 7-8 before the ceasefire announcement. After a brief dip to $650-675 on ceasefire headlines, prices recovered to $700 by April 9 and ended the week at $692/st per Argus Media. The Profercy World Nitrogen Index hit 352.95 on April 9, its highest since May 2022. Oil returned to $99-104 on the blockade re-escalation. India's IPL tendering 2.5 million tonnes of urea (closing April 15) adds fresh demand into an already structurally short market.
Evidence of lag: NOLA urea pulled back only 5.7% from its pre-ceasefire peak of $734 to $692, recovering almost immediately as vessel traffic through the Strait remained at a trickle - only 17 vessels on April 12 versus 130+ daily pre-war per Windward data. CF's stock, by contrast, dropped 14% from its $142 ATH to $121 during the same ceasefire window. As of April 14, CF has recovered only to $125, recouping roughly 3% of the 14% drawdown, despite the ceasefire's confirmed failure and the formal blockade declaration.
Thesis: The April 8 ceasefire triggered a sentiment-driven pullback in CF that was disproportionate to actual nitrogen market fundamentals. That ceasefire is now dead: talks failed April 12, the US declared a naval blockade April 13, and India is tendering 2.5 million tonnes of urea this week into a market where 35% of global seaborne supply is physically stranded. CF's cost structure is uniquely positioned to capture the full margin of $700+ urea. The $17 gap between CF's current price and its recent ATH represents a ceasefire discount that no longer has a basis in fact.
Counter-thesis: Goldman Sachs raised its CF price target to $132 but maintained a Neutral rating, reflecting a view that the nitrogen windfall is largely priced. Pakistan is actively mediating a second round of talks. If a credible ceasefire with enforcement mechanisms emerges before the India tender clears, urea could retrace sharply.
R2 flags: Verify real-time NOLA urea barge pricing post-blockade declaration. Confirm CF's Q1 realized urea pricing and margin against the $692-734 NOLA range. Check whether the DOJ fertilizer price-fixing inquiry creates regulatory overhang. Monitor India IPL tender outcome (closing April 15). May 6 earnings is a hard time stop.
