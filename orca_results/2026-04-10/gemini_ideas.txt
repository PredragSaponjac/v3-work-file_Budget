However, by April 9, the ceasefire proved precarious, the Strait of Hormuz remained de facto closed, and crude oil surged 6.8% back toward $100/bbl. While the commodity market quickly repriced the renewed geopolitical and supply risks, AAL shares stubbornly held their ceasefire-driven highs. As a highly leveraged and unhedged carrier, AAL should rapidly give back its premature relief rally to reflect the reality that fuel costs remain elevated.
