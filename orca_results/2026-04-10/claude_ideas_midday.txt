Cleanest expression: Valero is the largest independent US refiner by throughput capacity at 3.1 million barrels per day, running at 98% utilization, with a pure downstream refining exposure that directly captures the crack spread without upstream crude production revenue exposure that would offset the lower oil price benefit. Unlike MPC (Catlettsburg refinery shutdown, Mizuho forecasting a major Q1 EPS miss on MTM hedge losses) or PBF (negative TTM EBITDA, 22% short interest), Valero has the cleanest fundamental refining profile.
What already moved: WTI crude crashed 16.4% to $94.41 on April 8. Upstream E&P stocks Occidental, Devon, Diamondback, and ConocoPhillips fell 5.1%-7.5% on the ceasefire. The S&P 500 surged 2.51%. The 3-2-1 crack spread held above $41/barrel on April 9 per Benzinga, having spiked from below $20 in late 2025 to above $52 at peak and settling only modestly from its war-era high.
Evidence of lag: Pricing unverified - flag for R2
Thesis: The ceasefire-driven oil crash created a divergence between crude input costs (down sharply) and refined product output prices (still elevated on tight global product supply and the Strait of Hormuz remaining effectively closed with only 2-4 tanker transits versus the normal 135/day). Valero was sold off 4.71% on April 8 alongside upstream E&P names, despite the economic logic running in the opposite direction for a pure refiner. The crack spread at $41 directly flows to Valero's margins, and the stock now trades at 7-12x forward earnings with four major sell-side upgrades in the past two weeks. However, the lag between the crude crash and the refiner stock reaction has not been verified at sufficient granularity for R1 Accepted status, and the April 30 earnings report creates a practical interference concern within the repricing window.
Counter-thesis: The Benzinga article from April 9 already highlighted this exact refiner margin divergence, naming Valero among six refiners to watch - suggesting the trade is already visible to institutional participants and the lag may be narrower than it appears. Additionally, Valero's Port Arthur refinery (380K bbl/day, ~12% of total capacity) suffered an explosion on March 23 that destroyed a diesel hydrotreater control room, triggered a lawsuit, and required a full shutdown, which may partially explain the stock's underperformance relative to the margin improvement.
R2 flags: Verify Port Arthur refinery restart status and timeline; confirm whether Mizuho's MTM hedge loss thesis also applies to VLO; check whether the April 30 earnings report creates a binary event that dominates the repricing thesis; verify actual VLO price action relative to crack spread on April 8-10 at intraday granularity; check short interest and borrow conditions; confirm that gasoline and distillate product prices have not begun to compress faster than crude.
