Ticker | Company | Direction: CF | CF Industries Holdings | Bullish
Catalyst: US-Iran peace talks failed April 12; Trump ordered a US naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz effective April 13; China announced a de facto ban on sulfuric acid exports from May, compounding global fertilizer supply disruption during peak Northern Hemisphere planting season.
Catalyst timing: 2026-04-12 (talks failure and blockade announcement) / 2026-04-10 (China sulfuric acid ban) | Status: Active
Repricing window: days to 3 weeks
Entry trigger: CF holding above $125 with the ceasefire-driven selloff gap filling toward the pre-ceasefire $130+ area, confirmed by oil sustaining above $98 and no credible Hormuz reopening announcement
Invalidation condition: A verifiable US-Iran deal that includes a concrete, operational Hormuz reopening timeline with confirmed ship transits resuming at scale (50+ per day), which would re-establish the ceasefire selloff as legitimate; or CF failing to hold $120 on a session close despite oil remaining above $95
Target / repricing path: Recovery toward the $135-140 pre-ceasefire trading zone, representing a partial catch-up to the objective deterioration in the supply outlook relative to where CF was trading before the April 8 ceasefire selloff
Horizon / time stop: 1 to 3 weeks; review or exit earlier if a genuine diplomatic resolution emerges or if the ceasefire window produces a concrete shipping restart
Cleanest expression: CF is the largest US-listed pure-play nitrogen fertilizer producer, operating ammonia, granular urea, and UAN facilities in North America using low-cost domestic natural gas. Gulf-region competitors producing 46% of global urea are offline due to Hormuz. CF benefits directly from the supply gap and the cost advantage versus overseas producers facing spiking gas costs. More direct exposure than Nutrien (diversified across potash and retail) or Mosaic (phosphate-focused, margin-compressed by rising sulfur input costs).
What already moved: WTI crude surged to $99-104 on Monday April 13, up 4-8% on the blockade announcement. Urea prices at US Gulf ports are up over 25% since the war began in late February. QatarEnergy shut its QAFCO urea plant (5.6 million ton annual capacity). Egypt's urea production constrained by lost Israeli gas imports. Global urea supply contracted by an estimated 33%. China announced a sulfuric acid export ban from May, reported by Bloomberg on April 10, further tightening the global fertilizer chain.
Evidence of lag: CF dropped approximately $14 per share on April 8 (from the ~$135 area to ~$121) on ceasefire hopes - a selloff premised on Hormuz reopening and supply normalization. That premise has been explicitly disproven: talks failed April 12, Trump announced a blockade, and the strait remains at a trickle of traffic (fewer than 10 ships per day versus 135 pre-war). As of April 13, CF has recovered only $3-4 of the ~$14 decline to trade near $125, despite the supply outlook being objectively worse than before the ceasefire was announced. Oil and integrated energy names repriced 4-6% higher on the blockade; CF's recovery is muted by comparison.
Thesis: The ceasefire-driven selloff in CF was a bet on Hormuz normalization. That bet has failed - talks collapsed, Trump escalated to a formal naval blockade, and China's sulfuric acid ban layers a new independent supply shock on top of the existing nitrogen shortage. CF's cost position is strengthening, not weakening: US natural gas remains far cheaper than overseas alternatives, Gulf competitors remain offline, and spring planting season is actively driving demand. The stock's failure to recover the ceasefire gap despite worsening fundamentals represents a clean tactical lag that should compress as the market reprices the failed-deal reality.
Counter-thesis: Trump stated on Monday morning that Iran "still wants to make a deal," and the market's muted equity reaction suggests participants view the blockade as a negotiating tactic rather than a durable escalation. If a deal materializes within the remaining ~8 days of the ceasefire window, CF could give back today's gains and retest the $120 area. Additionally, Joe Terranova publicly sold CF on April 8, and analyst consensus is split (3 buy, 3 sell per Investing.com), signaling institutional skepticism that the war-driven premium is sustainable.
R2 flags: Verify CF's current realized urea and UAN pricing versus spot benchmarks. Confirm US natural gas cost advantage is holding. CF earnings May 6 - confirm timing overlap with thesis. Check whether CF hedged or pre-sold product at lower prices before the spike. Verify options positioning and short interest for crowding risk.
