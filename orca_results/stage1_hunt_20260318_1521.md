Why this company is the cleanest expression: Valero Energy is set to buy up to 6.5 million barrels of Venezuelan crude in March bound for its Gulf Coast refineries, making it the top foreign refiner of the OPEC country's oil. Valero has the second-largest U.S. refining network capable of processing Venezuelan heavy oil, with total refining capacity for Venezuelan crude oil around 240,000 bpd before an expansion at its 435,000-bpd refinery in Port Arthur, Texas. The convergence of multi-year-high crack spreads (crude-to-diesel, crude-to-gasoline) with expanding access to discounted heavy sour Venezuelan feedstock makes Valero the single-name where the earnings uplift is most concentrated and most immediate. Unlike integrated majors (XOM, CVX), Valero is a pure-play independent refiner, so crack margins flow almost entirely to the bottom line.

What already moved: Prices have surged about 40% during the U.S.-Iran war, reaching their highest levels since 2022, as shipping through the Strait has been severely disrupted. Brent rose 3.83% on Wednesday to close at $107.38 per barrel; U.S. oil prices ended the day at $96.32 per barrel. Diesel prices have jumped even more, topping $5 gallon this week, which is the highest level since December 2022. The average national gas price in the U.S. rose to $3.84 a gallon, up from $2.92 a month ago. LNG prices have risen even more sharply — by almost 60 percent — since the start of the war.
