Cleanest expression: CF Industries is the purest listed North American nitrogen fertilizer producer. Unlike Nutrien, which is diversified across potash and retail distribution, or Mosaic, which is a phosphate producer being squeezed by surging sulfur input costs, CF manufactures ammonia and urea directly from cheap domestic Henry Hub natural gas. This gives CF a structural cost advantage that widens as global nitrogen prices surge — the company operates at 97% ammonia utilization with a 46.7% EBITDA margin and captures the full scarcity premium without the input cost drag facing competitors.
What already moved: Global urea prices surged 53.7% in March per World Bank data. NOLA urea spot gapped approximately 35% higher. US Gulf ammonia settlements jumped $160 per ton to $775. The North American fertilizer price index hit $971 per short ton on April 7, a new record, up from $810 in mid-March. The Eurasia Group reported today that even a ceasefire would not restore supply for months due to damaged Gulf infrastructure and shipping logistics requiring 8+ weeks to restart.
Evidence of lag: CF stock hit its all-time high of $141.96 on March 30 but traded at approximately $130 on April 6 — an 8.5% decline from peak — even as the underlying fertilizer index continued surging from $810 to a record $971 over the same period. The pullback coincided with brief ceasefire optimism last week that has since reversed, with Bloomberg reporting today that Iran stopped negotiating a truce. Analyst consensus price targets cluster near $140, which may not yet reflect the April 7 record nitrogen pricing.
Thesis: The Hormuz closure has created the largest fertilizer supply disruption in years during peak spring application season. CF Industries, as the purest North American nitrogen producer with a domestic gas cost moat, captures the full margin expansion from record urea and ammonia prices. The stock pulled back approximately 8.5% from its all-time high during a brief ceasefire hope window that has now collapsed — Iran stopped negotiating today and Trump's 8 PM ET escalation deadline is tonight. The Eurasia Group's April 7 report confirms supply disruption will persist for months even with a ceasefire, supporting sustained elevated nitrogen pricing well beyond what the stock's pullback implies.
Counter-thesis: The DOJ opened a formal antitrust investigation into CF Industries and other nitrogen producers in early March, with Senator Hawley accusing CF of price gouging and demanding detailed pricing justifications. If the investigation escalates to enforcement action or consent decrees that constrain CF's pricing flexibility, the margin expansion thesis could be capped regardless of where spot nitrogen trades.
R2 flags: Verify CF's current forward hedging or committed sales at lower pre-crisis prices that could blunt near-term margin capture; confirm DOJ investigation status and timeline for any enforcement action; check whether analyst EPS estimates for Q1 2026 (reporting May 6) have been revised upward to reflect current nitrogen pricing; verify short interest and borrow conditions.
