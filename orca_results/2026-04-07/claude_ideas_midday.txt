The two largest ETF issuers in the world are simultaneously targeting Invesco's near-exclusive U.S. Nasdaq 100 franchise, signaling a permanent structural break of the licensing monopoly that has sustained QQQ's ~99.5% market share.

Cleanest expression: Invesco is the sole direct victim. QQQ ($376B) and QQQM ($70B) together represent roughly 20% of IVZ's total AUM and a disproportionately higher share of operating profit given passive ETF margin structure. No other listed name has this concentrated exposure to the Nasdaq 100 licensing franchise.
What already moved: IVZ fell 5.2% on Monday April 6 — the largest single-day decline since October — immediately after BlackRock's SEC filing became public. Bloomberg, Reuters, and Seeking Alpha all attributed the move directly to the competitive threat.
Evidence of lag: Pricing unverified — flag for R2
Thesis: BlackRock's filing on April 6 broke a decades-old exclusivity structure in which Invesco controlled nearly 100% of U.S.-listed Nasdaq 100 index ETF assets. The market's initial 5% reaction priced one competitor. State Street's filing the very next day transforms this from a bilateral challenge into an industry-wide assault — the #1 and #3 ETF issuers are both now targeting the same franchise. BlackRock will almost certainly price IQQ at a fraction of QQQ's 0.20% expense ratio, consistent with its iShares pricing strategy. The compounding signal — two filings in 24 hours — suggests the Nasdaq licensing moat has cracked permanently, and the franchise premium embedded in IVZ's valuation has not yet been fully repriced.
Counter-thesis: SPY has maintained over $600B in AUM for 30+ years despite IVV charging a fraction of the expense ratio. QQQ's massive options ecosystem, institutional liquidity, and trading infrastructure create switching costs that may protect the installed base even as marginal new flows divert. An Invesco spokesperson stated "There is only one QQQ," and the fund's entrenched position in trading desks and options market-making may prove more durable than the fee differential implies.
R2 flags: Verify IVZ's today-session reaction to the State Street news. Confirm QQQ/QQQM revenue and profit contribution to IVZ from latest filings. Check short borrow conditions. Confirm IVZ earnings date (April 28) does not create dominant interference. Monitor whether additional issuers file. Verify whether Nasdaq's licensing terms include any volume protections or exclusivity tail for Invesco.
