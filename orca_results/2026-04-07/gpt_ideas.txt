Evidence of lag: Pricing unverified — flag for R2
Thesis: Regeneron is the sole holdout among the 17 large drugmakers targeted by the administration, but it said on April 2 that it expects to announce a deal soon and avoid the new tariff regime. This is not Accepted because I do not yet have clean, explicit proof that REGN itself underreacted versus the prior relief reactions seen in peers.
Counter-thesis: The market may be right to wait because Regeneron is still the only one of the 17 large drugmakers without a signed agreement, so the exemption is not final and the eventual pricing concessions could offset part of the tariff-relief benefit.
R2 flags: Verify REGN's actual April 2-to-April 7 relative performance versus large-cap pharma peers, confirm final exemption terms, and monitor the April 29 earnings date as the next major company-specific event.
