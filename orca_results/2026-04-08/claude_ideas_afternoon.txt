Thesis: LYB rallied approximately 57% year-to-date on Iran war supply disruption that tightened global polyethylene markets and widened the U.S. ethane-versus-naphtha feedstock advantage. The ceasefire and 15-17% oil crash directly reverse both legs of that thesis: European and Asian naphtha crackers regain competitiveness as oil falls, and Strait of Hormuz reopening restores Middle East petrochemical supply. BofA explicitly warned that the rally reflected "overearnings" from temporary factors and downgraded to Underperform with a $55 target — implying approximately 30% downside from the April 7 close. The pre-war business was losing money and had already cut its dividend. If the ceasefire holds and supply normalizes, the war premium embedded in LYB should continue to unwind over the tactical window.
Counter-thesis: BofA's own note stated that petrochemical prices are expected to remain elevated through May-June even if the conflict ends soon, because physical supply normalization lags price signals. LYB's Q1 2026 results (due approximately April 24) will likely show strong war-premium earnings that could support the stock near-term. The ceasefire is explicitly described as fragile by Vice President Vance and multiple analysts, and Iran has already signaled it will regulate and potentially charge transit fees for Strait passage, meaning full supply normalization is not assured.

Q1 earnings approximately April 24 fall inside the repricing window and represent material interference risk — earnings could show strong war-premium results that temporarily support the stock.
